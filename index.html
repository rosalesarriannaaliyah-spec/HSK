Housekeeping Tools, Materials & Equipment

Scan the QR codes or use the search bar below to find a tool.

Broom

Definition: A cleaning tool with stiff fibers (bristles) attached to a handle, used for sweeping dirt and dust.

Uses: Sweeping floors, sidewalks, and other surfaces to remove dust, dirt, and debris.

Dustpan

Definition: A flat container with a handle, used with a broom to collect dust and dirt.

Uses: Collecting dirt, dust, and small debris after sweeping.

Mop

Definition: A tool with absorbent material attached to a handle, used for cleaning floors.

Uses: Wiping and cleaning wet or dirty floors, often with water and cleaning solution.

Bucket

Definition: A round container used for carrying water or cleaning solutions.

Uses: Holding water, detergents, or cleaning mixtures when mopping or washing.

Vacuum Cleaner

Definition: An electrical appliance that uses suction to remove dirt and dust from surfaces.

Uses: Cleaning carpets, floors, upholstery, and other surfaces efficiently.

Duster

Definition: A tool with soft feathers or fibers used for removing dust from surfaces.

Uses: Dusting furniture, shelves, appliances, and delicate items.

Sponge

Definition: A soft, porous cleaning material that absorbs liquid.

Uses: Washing dishes, cleaning surfaces, and absorbing spills.

Cleaning Gloves

Definition: Protective hand coverings made of rubber, latex, or plastic.

Uses: Protecting hands from chemicals, dirt, and germs during cleaning.

Scrub Brush

Definition: A brush with stiff bristles used for scrubbing tough dirt and stains.

Uses: Cleaning floors, tiles, sinks, and other hard surfaces.

Spray Bottle

Definition: A plastic bottle with a trigger nozzle for spraying liquids.

Uses: Spraying water, disinfectants, or cleaning solutions on surfaces.
